Ummet Ozcan

In an age where very few artists break the mold and truly shine through as unique and innovative, Dutch star Ummet Ozcan is one of the exceptions to the rule. Not only because of his advanced studio knowledge and the impressive string of dance floor hits this resulted in, it’s also thanks to his enduring presence at worldwide mainstages these last years. A success story that’s about to enter new grounds. Growing up in the known epicentre of dance music, The Netherlands, it isn’t difficult for Ozcan to plunge into dance music at an early age. “The EDM scene has been very big here since I was a teenager. We’ve had dance music playing on radio stations for over 20 years now”, he explains. So, as soon as he’s good and ready, music starts to come out of Ozcan’s studio. It won’t take long before he gets the attention he deserves. Since achieving his first major success in 2011 with ‘Reboot’, which spends seven weeks at #1 on Beatport, the veteran DJ’s major hits have been sampled in his peers’ radio podcasts and played at festival mainstages all over the world. Catch him playing live on 27th December at Gymkhana grounds, IIT Bombay

Leprous

Leprous is a progressive metal band from Notodden, Norway, formed in 2001. Leprous' style is typically classified as progressive metal or progressive rock and it singled out for its progressive elements, peculiar harmonies, singing style, and blending of different genres. Their sound has also been categorized as experimental rock, avant-garde metal, and pop. However, the band's members do not affiliate themselves with a specific genre. Einar Solberg, one of the band’s founding members, claims inspiration from Radiohead, Massive Attack, Arvo Pärt, Susanne Sundfør, Behemoth, and The Prodigy in addition to more analogous progressive bands such as Porcupine Tree and The Dillinger Escape Plan. In a time where progressive music is emerging and there are so many options available to new listeners, it takes serious guts and personality to turn heads anymore. Norway’s Leprous has done just that in recent years, and it has been such a joy to watch them emerge into the limelight of awareness within the progressive community. It is no wonder, however, as Leprous continues to mature and to cling ever closer to their hearts when crafting new music. Leprous is a visionary band that defies genre labelling, not just in the style, but also in their attitude. Leprous, however, is not a band that takes success resting on their hind quarters, as they believe in pushing boundaries, learning from their mistakes, and following their instincts. This was proved when they released their new album “Malina” this summer, and offered a left hook to the progressive world.

aswekeepsearching

Aswekeepsearching has been turning heads and perking up ears over the world for a while now, spreading a message of positivity and good times in a way only they can, while keeping their feet planted firmly on the ground. Their music is an aural painting, cathartic and unbiased, vividly colourful and infinitely kind. From the first bar of the music till the curtain drops, it is apparent that the four members, fuelled by idealism and the drive to build something beautiful, are set out to move mountains. The albums also see the use of instruments like the Violin, Viola, Cello, Sitar and the Tabla, having collaborated with like-minded artists on some songs, who've added their own unique colour to the bands soundscape.

Papon

Angaraag Mahanta, popularly known as Papon, is a singer, multi-instrumentalist, composer and record producer. Blessed with a voice that’s smooth and soulful at the same time, his innovative experiments are rich with ambient electronica, acoustic folk, electro ghazals to classical sounds. Papon can sing effortlessly in Hindi, Punjabi, Marathi, Tamil, Bengali, and Gujarati language. In Bollywood, Papon’s rise to fame was with the songs like ‘Moh Moh Ke Dhage’ from Dum Laga Ke Haisha, ‘Kyon’ from Barfi ,‘Jiyein Kyun’ from the movie Dum Maro Dum, ‘Labon Ka Karobar’ for the movie Befikre etc. In 2018, Papon has lent his voice to songs in movie Sanju, Raid, Karwaan, Sui Dhaaga. Papon has been a pioneer of Indian independent music & has co-composed songs like ‘Har Ek Baat’ and ‘Naina Laagey’ with pioneering producers Midival Punditz. He has also collaborated with renowned composer-multi instrumentalist Karsh Kale on the track ‘Absence’ for Karsh Kale’s fifth studio album. He was awarded as the Best Male Playback Singer 2016 at Star Screen Awards, Mirchi Music Awards, TOIFA, GIMA, IIFA-Madrid. Papon was also nominated for Best Male Playback Singer at the FILMFARE Awards and Zee Cine Awards in 2016. His concert on 29th December will be surely something to look forward for all the Indian music lovers!

After Acoustics

After Acoustics is a Pune based folk rock/country music band, established in 2014. The band's music comprises of peppy, foot tapping country songs that make the audience sing along with gusto, leading them to often be compared to Mumford and Sons. What sets them apart is their ridiculous enthusiasm and energy. It is physically impossible to watch them live and NOT leave with a giant smile plastered on your face.

Tarq Bowen

Tarq's unique blend of Blues, Soul and hard-hitting slide guitar interfaced with powerful yet gentle dynamic ability shows an understanding and skill far beyond his years. This has attracted the attention of Nick Mason (Pink Floyd) who recorded Tarq's debut single, remaining a big avid supporter of his music. Last year Tarq had the rare opportunity to be personally invited by Tony Visconti, to play alongside Nitin Sawhney and Stewart Copeland of The Police, in a 6 piece documentary which led to Tarq flying to L.A. to work with veteran producer Larry Klein. Whilst Tarq draws inspiration from the likes of Debussy, Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan and Chet Baker, his sound has been compared more accurately to artists such as Jeff Buckley and Howlin Wolf. Playing with his live band Tarq's sound invites the audience on a magnetic journey infused with energy and soul, leaving a unique memory that will evolve every time you see him. Tarq will feature his international Trio at the Mood Indigo festival, Tarq Bowen (UK) on Vocals & Guitars, Phil Wildenhues (NL) on Drums and Marc-Inti (BOL) on E-Bass. As a special guest Amit Mishra (IND) will join them on Tabla.

Leprous

Leprous is a progressive metal band from Notodden, Norway, formed in 2001. Leprous' style is typically classified as progressive metal or progressive rock and it singled out for its progressive elements, peculiar harmonies, singing style, and blending of different genres. Their sound has also been categorized as experimental rock, avant-garde metal, and pop. However, the band's members do not affiliate themselves with a specific genre. Einar Solberg, one of the band’s founding members, claims inspiration from Radiohead, Massive Attack, Arvo Pärt, Susanne Sundfør, Behemoth, and The Prodigy in addition to more analogous progressive bands such as Porcupine Tree and The Dillinger Escape Plan. In a time where progressive music is emerging and there are so many options available to new listeners, it takes serious guts and personality to turn heads anymore. Norway’s Leprous has done just that in recent years, and it has been such a joy to watch them emerge into the limelight of awareness within the progressive community. It is no wonder, however, as Leprous continues to mature and to cling ever closer to their hearts when crafting new music. Leprous is a visionary band that defies genre labelling, not just in the style, but also in their attitude. Leprous, however, is not a band that takes success resting on their hind quarters, as they believe in pushing boundaries, learning from their mistakes, and following their instincts. This was proved when they released their new album “Malina” this summer, and offered a left hook to the progressive world.
